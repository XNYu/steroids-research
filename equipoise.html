1. Basic Information

Chemical Identity

  • Chemical Name: Boldenone undecylenate (boldenone undecenoate)
  • IUPAC Name: [(8R,9S,10R,13S,14S,17S)-10,13-Dimethyl-3-oxo-6,7,8,9,11,12,14,15,16,17-decahydrocyclopenta[a]phenanthren-17-yl] undec-10-enoate
  • Trade Names: Equipoise, Parenabol, Boldane, Ganabol
  • CAS Number: 13103-34-9
  • Molecular Formula: C30H44O3
  • Molecular Weight: 452.679 g/mol

    • Dosage Information

  • Veterinary Dosage: 1.1mg/kg weekly (horses)
  • Performance Dosage: 200-600mg per week
  • Cycle Length: 10-16 weeks
  • Administration: Intramuscular injection (IM)

    • Pharmacokinetics

  • Bioavailability: 100% via IM injection
  • Half-life: 14 days (long-acting)
  • Duration of Action: 3-4 weeks
  • Peak Blood Levels: 5-7 days post-injection
  • Detection Time: Up to 5 months (longest of common AAS)

    • Historical Context

  • Development: 1950s by Ciba Pharmaceuticals
  • Original Purpose: Human clinical use (anemia treatment)
  • Human Medical Use: 1960s-1970s (brand name Parenabol)
  • Veterinary Adoption: Late 1970s (Squibb, brand name Equipoise)
  • Current Status: Primarily veterinary medicine, illicit human use

    • Legal Status

  • US Classification: Schedule III controlled substance
  • UK Classification: Class C drug (POM)
  • Canada: Schedule IV
  • Veterinary Use: Still approved in Australia and US
  • Human Medical Use: Discontinued worldwide

    6. Deep Analysis of Specific Side Effects

    6.1 Anxiety and Mood Disturbances

    #### Why It Occurs

    The anxiety associated with EQ is multifactorial:

    1. Central Nervous System Stimulation: Boldenone metabolites affect neurotransmitter systems

    2. Catecholamine Interaction: Increased norepinephrine activity

    3. Hormonal Fluctuations: Testosterone suppression creates imbalance

    4. Sleep Disruption: Affects next-day anxiety levels

    5. Individual Sensitivity: Genetic differences in CNS response

    #### What Users Report

  • General restlessness and inability to relax
  • Racing thoughts, particularly at night
  • Increased heart rate, palpitations
  • Sleep difficulties (falling/staying asleep)
  • Heightened startle response
  • Occasional panic attacks in severe cases

    • #### Prevalence Data

    SymptomPrevalenceNotes
    General Anxiety15-25%Most common
    Sleep Disturbance20-30%Related to anxiety
    Panic Attacks5-10%Severe cases
    Mood Swings15-20%Irritability, agitation

    #### How to Manage

    Prevention:

  • Start with lower doses (200-300mg/week)
  • Gradual dose increases over 2-3 weeks
  • Avoid high doses (>600mg/week) if anxiety-prone
  • Adequate sleep hygiene
  • Limit caffeine and stimulants

    • Treatment:

  • Dose reduction or discontinuation
  • Anxiolytics (short-term use only)
  • Magnesium supplementation (400-600mg daily)
  • Ashwagandha (adaptogen)
  • Regular cardiovascular exercise
  • Meditation/relaxation techniques

    • #### Scientific Explanation

    Research suggests that boldenone and its metabolites can cross the blood-brain barrier and interact with GABA, dopamine, and norepinephrine systems. The 1,2-dehydrogenation creates a compound with enhanced CNS penetration compared to testosterone. Individual differences in enzyme activity (particularly CYP450 isoforms) likely explain the variable response.

    #### Why It's a Concern

    1. Viscosity Risk: Excess RBC mass increases blood thickness

    2. Thrombotic Potential: Higher clotting risk

    3. Hypertension: Increased blood volume

    4. Heart Strain: Elevated cardiac workload

    4. Dose Adjustment: Reduce EQ if values elevated

    6.3 Injection Protocol Optimization

    #### Injection Technique

    Recommended Sites:

  • Ventrogluteal (preferred - avoids sciatic nerve)
  • Deltoid (alternative, less comfortable)
  • Vastus lateralis (good alternative)

    • Needle Selection:

  • Gauge: 22-25G (thicker for viscous oil)
  • Length: 1.5 inch (standard for IM)
  • Needle exchange for reduced pain

    • #### Pain Management

    1. Warm the Oil: Roll vial in hands or warm water

    2. Slow Injection: 1-2 minutes for 1mL

    3. Deep IM: Proper technique to avoid subcutaneous

    4. Massage: Light massage after injection

    5. Rotate Sites: Never inject same location twice

    #### Schedule Optimization

  • Weekly: Standard (once per week)
  • Split Dosing: 300mg twice weekly for stability
  • Injection Day: Consistent time each week
