1. Basic Information

Chemical Identity

  • Chemical Name: Nandrolone phenylpropionate
  • IUPAC Name: [(8R,9S,10R,13S,14S,17S)-13-methyl-3-oxo-2,6,7,8,9,10,11,12,14,15,16,17-dodecahydro-1H-cyclopenta[a]phenanthren-17-yl] 3-phenylpropanoate
  • Trade Names: Durabolin, NPP
  • CAS Number: 62-90-2
  • Molecular Formula: C27H34O3
  • Molecular Weight: 406.57 g/mol

    • Dosage Information

  • Therapeutic Dosage: 25-100 mg every 3-7 days (historically)
  • Performance Dosage: 300-600 mg per week
  • Cycle Length: 8-16 weeks
  • Administration: Intramuscular injection (IM)

    • Pharmacokinetics

  • Bioavailability: Similar to other IM nandrolone esters
  • Half-life: 2-3 days (shorter than decanoate)
  • Duration of Action: 10-14 days (in terms of detectable effects)
  • Peak Blood Levels: 24-48 hours post-injection
  • Injection Frequency: Every 2-3 days for stable blood levels

    • Historical Context

  • Introduction: 1959 (first nandrolone ester)
  • Developer: Organon International
  • Original Purpose: Clinical therapy for muscle wasting, anemia
  • Current Status: Limited availability, primarily underground market

    • 2. Mechanism of Action

    Core Pharmacological Profile

    NPP shares the same active parent compound (nandrolone) with Deca Durabolin, but differs in its pharmacokinetic profile due to the phenylpropionate ester.

    2.1 Ester Chemistry and Release Kinetics

    Phenylpropionate Ester Characteristics:

  • Carbon Length: ~6-7 carbons (aromatic ring adds complexity)
  • Hydrophobicity: Moderate (less than decanoate)
  • Esterase Sensitivity: Higher (faster hydrolysis)
  • Oil Solubility: Similar to other nandrolone esters

    • Release Mechanism:

    1. IM injection forms depot in muscle tissue

    2. Gradual hydrolysis by local esterases

    3. Steady release over 2-3 days

    4. Peak plasma concentrations within 24-48 hours

    5. Smoother fluctuation compared to shorter esters

    2.2 Androgen Receptor Activity

  • Relative AR Affinity: 154-155% of testosterone
  • 5Œ±-Dihydronandrolone Formation: Inactivated in androgenic tissues
  • Anabolic Potency: 11:1 ratio (myotrophic:androgenic)
  • Tissue Selectivity: Similar to nandrolone decanoate

    • 2.3 Hormonal Feedback Pathways

    Identical to Nandrolone Decanoate:

  • HPG axis suppression via negative feedback
  • Reduced GnRH, LH, FSH secretion
  • Endogenous testosterone suppression (57-70% at typical doses)
  • Prolactin elevation mechanisms unchanged

    • 2.4 Key Differences from Deca

    1. Faster Onset: Active blood levels achieved more quickly

    2. Shorter Duration: Requires more frequent injections

    3. Quicker Clearance: Faster elimination if adverse effects occur

    4. Less Accumulation: Lower tissue reservoir effect

    5. More Control: Easier to adjust doses mid-cycle

    6. Deep Analysis of Specific Side Effects

    6.1 Prolactin Management with NPP

    #### Why It's Important

    Despite shorter ester, NPP still significantly elevates prolactin through identical mechanisms as Deca. The frequent injection schedule means:

  • More opportunities for prolactin spikes
  • Need for consistent prolactin management
  • Faster feedback on management effectiveness

    • #### Management Strategies

    Pharmacological:

  • Cabergoline 0.25-0.5mg twice weekly (minimum)
  • Increase to 0.5mg 2-3x weekly if prolactin elevated
  • Bromocriptine as alternative (less effective, more nausea)

    • Dosing Schedule:

  • Inject cabergoline mid-week and weekend
  • Maintain consistent timing for stable dopamine levels
  • Monitor symptoms (libido, mood, discharge)

    • Symptom Monitoring:

  • Track sexual function weekly
  • Watch for mood changes
  • Note any nipple sensitivity or discharge
  • Adjust dose based on symptoms, not just bloodwork

    • 6.2 Injection Protocol Optimization

    #### Frequency Guidelines

    Optimal Schedule:

  • Every other day (EOD): Most stable blood levels
  • Every 3 days (E3D): Acceptable for experienced users
  • Twice weekly: Minimum for decent stability

    • #### Injection Site Rotation

    Recommended Sites:

  • Gluteus maximus (largest muscle, slowest absorption)
  • Deltoid (faster absorption, convenient)
  • Quadriceps (good alternative)

    • Rotation Strategy:

  • Never inject same site twice in one week
  • Alternate left/right for each injection
  • Track injection locations to avoid overuse

    • #### Pain Minimization

    Techniques:

  • Warm the oil (roll between hands, warm water)
  • Use larger gauge needles (22-23G)
  • Inject slowly (1-2 minutes)
  • Deep IM injection (ventrogluteal preferred)
  • Massage area after injection
